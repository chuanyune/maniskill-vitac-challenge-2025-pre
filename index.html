Overview

The ManiSkill-Vitac Challenge 2025 builds upon last year's ManiSkill-Vitac-Challenge-2024, which focused on tactile sensing. This year, we are offering three distinct tracks, each corresponding to different skills and capabilities.

To the best of our knowledge, this is the first open challenge in this field. We hope it will bring together researchers from around the world, fostering collaboration between experts in tactile sensing and policy learning, and driving the development of advanced manipulation skill learning frameworks based on tactile sensing.

The ManiSkill-ViTac Challenge features:

  • Real-world robot evaluation
  • Tactile vision fusion manipulation
  • An open-source general-purpose tactile sensor physics simulator
  • Two high-precision manipulation tasks that closely resemble real-world applications

Challenge Format

This challenge consists of three independent tracks. Participating teams can choose to compete in 1-3 tracks when registering. Each track is completely separate and does not interfere with the others. The evaluation criteria and advancement standards for each track are different.

Track 1: Vision-based-tactile

  • Input Information: Tactile information only
  • Stage 1: Ranking based on evaluation results in simulation
  • Stage 2: Only teams advancing from Stage 1 participate, submitting their best checkpoint
  • Scoring Criteria: Success rate of Task 1 * 50 + Success rate of Task 2 * 50

Track 2: Tactile vision fusion

  • Input Information: Tactile information + RealSense visual information
  • Stage 1: Ranking based on evaluation results in simulation
  • Stage 2: Only teams advancing from Stage 1 participate, submitting their best checkpoint
  • Scoring Criteria: Success rate * 100

Track 3: Sensor structure design

  • Task Type: Subjective task
  • Design Content: Design the shape of the sensor's silicone structure using the provided method
  • Evaluation Method: Assessed by the organizers

Participation Guidelines

  • Teams can choose to participate in 1-3 tracks
  • Each track is conducted independently and does not affect the others
  • Stage 2 of Track 1 and Track 2 is only open to teams that succeed in Stage 1

Tracks

Here are the three main tracks for our challenge. Each track is designed to test different skills and abilities.

Track 1: Vision-based-Tactile Manipulation

Thetrack includes two tasks: peg insertion and lock opening. Similar to the ManiSkill challenge, we focus on evaluating the policyâ€™s generalizability. Each task includes multiple objects of different shapes (e.g., the peg can be a cuboid or a trapezoid, and keys can have two or three teeth), which are unknown during evaluation. Therefore, the policy needs to be able to generalize to different plugs and keys.

Track 2: Tactile-Vision-Fusion Manipulation

In this task, the experimental setup remains unchanged. However, compared to the only tactile task, a Realsense D415 is used to provide visual information, and the distance between the peg and the hole is increased. The gripper needs to determine which hole to insert the peg into based on the shape of the gripped peg. Since the tactile information remains the same during the approach to the hole, the task primarily relies on visual data for decision-making.

Track 3: Sensor Structure Design

This section is subjectively evaluated, where teams design sensors following our tutorial and submit either SLDPRT or STL files. The judges will score this, and it accounts for 20% of the total score.
